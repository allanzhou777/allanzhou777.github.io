And as it turns out, we had a unit later that year on maps. Apparently, there are also maps that use color intensity to show something, like population density or per-capita stuff. Anyhow, I found it pretty fascinating.

I also found it very intersting how there isn't a single map that is 100% accurate if you draw it on 2d paper. I guess this is somewhat reasonable to think, but once you think about it, map projections do serve a pretty improtant role.

The first map we learned was the Mercator projection, which is the most commonly used one, and also the one that my teacher dislikes the most. After a bit of research, you'll see that this map was made by the British Empire, and exaggerated the amount of land that they occupied (mainly by enlarging the Northern hemisphere). Others argue that even though it is the most popular, it just so happens to perpetuate the view that developing countries are inferior.
