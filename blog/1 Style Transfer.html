Part 1: Style transfer -- the basics

What is style transfer

At the barebones level, style transfer allows you to recreate the content of an image in the style of another. If youâ€™ve ever imagined what a photo might look like were it painted by a famous artist, then you're thinking of exactly what style transfer does.

Central Austin has a famous mural known as the [Greetings from Austin Mural]. What if, instead of being drawn by a local Austinite, this mural was drawn by Van Gogh in the style of his world-renown Starry Night? Well, style transfer allows you to create exactly that.

Modes and types of style transfer

Alright, so at this point, we know what style transfer can do. More generally though, style transfer is an example of image stylization, an image processing and manipulation technique utilized in the broader field of non-photorealistic rendering.

One may presume that lots of 'style-reference' images are require for the computer to learn the style utilized by the creator/author. However, in recent years, a new approach, neural style transfer (NST), has changed this. NST employs deep neural networks to power these transformations, extracting quantitative content and style feature data so that we can quantify how well the style transfer is working without the explicit image pairs. With this improved approach, only a single style reference image is needed for a successful the neural network. Very cool indeed

Why is style transfer important?

Lets be honest, not everybody is an artist. And not everybody can be convinced that they can draw things, (I for one, still don't consider myself an even remotely good artist even though everybody I've met has said otherwise). However, I make up for this in my technical skills. In recent years, advances in technology, like style transfer, have enabled more people to create enjoyable (and pretty aesthetic) art.

This can easily be a creative asset, too. As a side project, you could use this technology to create different interpretations/renditions of famous masterpieces. And sometimes, this can even be commercially valuable. Recently, Christie's featured AI artwork sold at an auction for over $430,000, which is over 45 times the average price of artwork created by technology.

With technology improving and advancing every day, style transfer can expect to see endless opportunities in design, content creation, creativity-related development, and, of course, commercial art.

  • Virtual reality (pretty obvious here)

Or course, this is nowhere near an exhaustive list, but it does hint at the massive potential of this technology.
